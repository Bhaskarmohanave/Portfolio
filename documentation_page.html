Introduction

The Chandrayaan missions are a series of lunar exploration projects initiated by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). These missions aim to explore the Moon, study its surface, and discover potential resources such as water ice. Through these missions, ISRO has made remarkable contributions to space exploration and has demonstrated India's growing capabilities in space technology.

Mission-1

  • Launched on: October 22, 2008
  • Launch Vehicle: PSLV-C11
  • Objectives: To orbit the Moon, map its surface, and study the mineral composition
  • Key Achievements:

    • Water Discovery: Chandrayaan-1 confirmed the presence of water molecules on the Moon's surface, a groundbreaking discovery that changed our understanding of the Moon.

    • Orbiter: The spacecraft successfully orbited the Moon and conducted detailed mapping of the surface and its mineral composition.

    • Scientific Contributions: The data collected provided insights into the Moon's topography, mineralogy, and the composition of its exosphere.

  • Status: The spacecraft lost communication with ISRO in August 2009, several months before its planned mission end, but the data collected before that was valuable.

Mission-1

  • Launched on: July 22, 2019
  • Launch Vehicle: GSLV Mk III-M1
  • Objectives: Chandrayaan-2 was designed to land near the Moon’s south pole and deploy a rover to study the surface in more detail. The orbiter aimed to continue mapping and studying the Moon’s surface and exosphere.
  • Key Achievements:

    • Soft Landing Attempt: Chandrayaan-2 aimed to make a soft landing on the Moon's surface, but the lander Vikram lost communication during the descent.

    • Orbiter: The orbiter successfully entered lunar orbit and has been sending high-resolution images and data about the Moon's surface, including details of its mineral composition and exosphere.

    • Scientific Contributions: Chandrayaan-2 has contributed to our understanding of the Moon's topography, mineralogy, and the presence of water ice in the polar regions.

  • Status: The orbiter is still operational and continues to send data to ISRO.

Mission-3

  • Launched on: July 14, 2023
  • Launch Vehicle: GSLV Mk III
  • Objectives: Chandrayaan-3 was designed to successfully land near the Moon’s south pole and deploy a rover to study the surface and explore resources like water ice.
  • Key Achievements:

    • Lander Success: The Vikram Lander of Chandrayaan-3 successfully completed a soft landing on the Moon’s surface, marking a significant achievement for ISRO.

    • Rover Deployment: The Pragyan Rover was successfully deployed and began conducting scientific experiments, including analyzing soil samples and studying the Moon’s composition.

    • Scientific Contributions: The rover sent back critical data on the Moon’s surface composition, water ice potential, and other key resources necessary for future lunar exploration.

  • Status: The mission is considered a significant achievement, with both the lander and rover performing as expected.

Conclusion

  • Chandrayaan-1 successfully mapped the lunar surface and made the historic discovery of water molecules on the Moon.
  • Chandrayaan-1 while facing a setback with its lander, continues to provide valuable scientific data through its orbiter.
  • Chandrayaan-1 has been a major success, with both the lander and rover achieving their objectives and providing critical data for future lunar exploration.

These missions are not just about technological achievements but are also paving the way for India to play a prominent role in space exploration, laying the groundwork for future lunar missions and beyond.
